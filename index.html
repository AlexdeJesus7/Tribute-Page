Nelson Mandela

The Man Who Fought for Freedom

Here is a timeline of some key moments in his life:

  • 1918 - Birth in Mvezo, South Africa:

    • On July 18, Nelson Mandela is born into a family of Thembu tribal royalty.

  • 1943 - Joining the African National Congress (ANC):

    • Mandela joins the ANC, marking the beginning of his journey into anti-apartheid activism.

  • 1962 - Arrest and Life Sentence:

    • Mandela is arrested and sentenced to life imprisonment for anti-apartheid activities.

  • 1990 - Release after 27 Years in Prison:

    • On February 11, Mandela is released after nearly three decades in prison.

  • 1993 - Nobel Peace Prize:

    • Mandela, along with Frederik de Klerk, is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for the peaceful transition to the end of apartheid.

  • 1994 - First Democratic Elections:

    • Mandela becomes the first black president of South Africa in the democratic elections.

  • 1995 - Rugby World Cup:

    • Mandela uses the Rugby World Cup as an opportunity to unite the post-apartheid nation.

  • 1999 - Retirement from Active Politics:

    • Mandela retires from active politics after a single presidential term.

  • 2001 - Prostate Cancer Diagnosis:

    • Mandela is diagnosed with prostate cancer but continues his philanthropic work.

  • 2004 - Nelson Mandela Foundation:

    • He establishes the Nelson Mandela Foundation, dedicated to promoting human rights and reconciliation.

  • 2013 - Death in Johannesburg, South Africa:

    • On December 5, Mandela passes away, leaving a lasting legacy of the fight for justice and equality.

  • Enduring Legacy:

    • Nelson Mandela's legacy is remembered as a testimony to resilience, reconciliation, and inspirational leadership that transcends the borders of South Africa.

    Nelson Mandela's life is a testament to perseverance and dedication to justice, and his impact continues to be a source of inspiration for future generations.

“He was fearless. When you make a decision that you are prepared to die for a cause, what have you got to be afraid of….Despair disappears from people who are on the brink of losing their lives. And if they have accepted it, that’s it, do your worst.”

-- one of Mandela's defense lawyers George Bizos
